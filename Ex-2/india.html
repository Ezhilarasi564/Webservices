India

Geography
India is the world's seventh largest country, with a land area of 3,287,263 square kilometers. It's located in the northern hemisphere, bordered by the Himalayas to the north, the Bay of Bengal to the east, and the Arabian Sea to the west.
Government
India is a parliamentary democracy with a federal structure. The president is the constitutional head of the country, while the prime minister leads the Council of Ministers, which advises the president.
Economy
India is one of the world's fastest growing economies, with a nominal GDP of $2,250 billion. The economy is based on services, industries, information technology, farming, and outsourcing.
Culture
India has a rich cultural heritage and is home to many historic trade routes and empires. The Mongols ruled India from 1527 to 1707, and built the Taj Mahal during this time.
Wildlife
India is home to many animals, including tigers, lions, leopards, elephants, rhinoceroses, bears, deer, monkeys, and cows. The peacock is the national bird, and the cobra is the best-known reptile.
Climate
India has a variety of climates, from tropical rain forests in the south to snow-covered Himalayan heights in the north.
Agriculture
India is self-sufficient in agricultural production, and 56% of the land is used for agriculture.
History
